SECTION A: FUNDAMENTAL THEORY
================================================================================

A.1  ACTION
----------
S = ∫ d⁴x √(-g) [ R/(16πG) − (a₀²/8πG)·F(y) − ¼·e^(-2Φ/c²)·F_μν·F^μν + L_m ]

A.2  LAGRANGIAN
---------------
F(y) = y − 2√y + 2·ln(1 + √y)

where y = |∇Φ|² / a₀²

The three terms encode three structural levels of the dual tetrad:

  Term 1:  y              k² = 16 level    Full interaction        Even parity
  Term 2:  −2√y           k  = 4  level    Face propagation        ODD parity (s = −1)
  Term 3:  2·ln(1+√y)     k⁰ = 1  level    Field origin            Even parity

A.3  CONSTITUTIVE LAW
---------------------
Variation of F(y) yields:  μ(x) = x / (1 + x)     where x = |∇Φ| / a₀

This is the "simple interpolating function" in the MOND literature, but
here it is DERIVED, not assumed. It follows uniquely from F(y).

A.4  FIELD EQUATION (SPHERICAL SYMMETRY)
----------------------------------------
μ(|∇Φ|/a₀) · |∇Φ| = g_N

Solving for |∇Φ| = g:

  g · g/(g + a₀) = g_N · a₀ · a₀ ... wait, let me be precise.

  μ(g/a₀) · g = g_N
  [g/a₀ / (1 + g/a₀)] · g = g_N
  g² / (a₀ + g) = g_N
  g² − g_N · g − g_N · a₀ = 0

  g = ½ [ g_N + √(g_N² + 4·g_N·a₀) ]

This is the EXACT algebraic solution. No approximation. No series expansion.

A.5  ACCELERATION SCALE
-----------------------
a₀ = k² · G · m_e / r_e²

Substituting:
  k  = 4                        (tetrahedral face count)
  G  = 6.674000e-11 m³ kg⁻¹ s⁻²
  m_e = 9.109000e-31 kg            (electron mass, CODATA 2018)
  r_e = 2.818000e-15 m             (classical electron radius, CODATA 2018)

  a₀ = 16 × 6.6740e-11 × 9.1090e-31 / (2.8180e-15)²
     = 1.224884e-10 m/s²

  Compare: Milgrom's empirical a₀ = 1.2 × 10⁻¹⁰ m/s²
  Ratio: 1.0207

  This is NOT fitted. It is computed from topology + CODATA constants.


A.6  LIMITING BEHAVIOR
-----------------------
  High acceleration (g_N >> a₀):
    g → g_N                          [Newtonian recovery]
    v = √(g_N · r) = √(GM/r)        [Keplerian decline]

  Low acceleration (g_N << a₀):
    g → √(g_N · a₀)                 [Deep MOND regime]
    v⁴ = G · M · a₀                  [Flat rotation / BTFR]

  Transition occurs at:
    g_N = a₀  →  r_transition = √(GM/a₀)

    For M = 10¹⁰ M_sun:  r_t = 3.4 kpc
    For M = 10⁸  M_sun:  r_t = 0.3 kpc


================================================================================
SECTION B: FIELD ORIGIN TREATMENT
================================================================================

B.1  THE PROBLEM
-----------------
Inside the galaxy's gravity envelope throat (r < R_throat), we are inside
the field origin. At this topological state (s = 0), both tetrahedra of
the dual tetrad coincide. The odd-parity term (−2√y) in F(y) has not
propagated outward. The field origin IS the source.

If we apply the full DTG formula (derived from the s = −1 spatial
projection) inside the field origin, we OVERSHOOT the inner rotation
curve. This overshoot occurs in M33, NGC 2403, and DDO 154.

B.2  PHYSICAL REASONING
-------------------------
At r = 0: You are AT the field origin. The scalar field Φ is the source
potential itself. There is no "propagated field" distinct from the source.
Gravity should be Newtonian: g = g_N.

At r = R_throat: You have crossed the throat of the gravity envelope.
The field has fully separated from the source. The scalar enhancement is
fully operational. Gravity follows the full DTG equation: g = g_DTG.

Between r = 0 and r = R_throat: The field progressively activates.

B.3  IMPLEMENTATION
--------------------
  For r < R_throat:
    blend(r) = r / R_throat    [linear activation, 0 at center, 1 at throat]
    g(r) = g_N(r) + blend(r) · [g_DTG(r) − g_N(r)]

  For r ≥ R_throat:
    g(r) = g_DTG(r)            [full covariant completion]

  This means:
    At r = 0:        g = g_N                    (pure Newtonian)
    At r = R_throat: g = g_DTG                  (full DTG)
    At r >> R_throat: g → √(g_N · a₀)          (deep MOND)

B.4  THROAT RADIUS
-------------------
  R_throat = (4/13) · R_env = 0.3077 · R_env

  4/13 = 17/13 − 1 = (k²+1)/(kd+1) − 1

  Where:
    17 = k² + 1 = 16 + 1 = total coupling channels (includes field origin)
    13 = k·d + 1 = 12 + 1 = spatial coupling channels (includes field origin)
    4/13 = excess fraction from field origin topology

  R_env is the galaxy's baryonic horizon: the outermost extent of the
  baryonic mass distribution (typically the HI disk edge).

B.5  CRITICAL NOTE ON THE BLEND
---------------------------------
The linear blend g_N → g_DTG is a simplification. The true transition
should follow from evaluating F(y) at intermediate s-parameter values
between s = 0 (field origin) and s = −1 (spatial projection). The
blend function should ultimately be derived, not assumed. However, the
linear blend captures the essential physics: suppressed enhancement at
the center, full enhancement at the throat.

The blend does NOT work well for the Milky Way because ALL MW data
points are inside or near the throat (R_throat = 15 kpc, data from
5-25 kpc). For the MW, the blend suppresses the DTG enhancement
across the entire observed range, making the fit worse. The MW
underprediction is better explained by baryonic mass underestimate.

SECTION C: RECURSIVE STRUCTURAL ACCELERATION
================================================================================

C.1  PHYSICAL BASIS
---------------------
Every star in a galaxy has its own gravity envelope with k = 4 tetrahedral
closure. Each stellar field origin contributes 4/13 = 30.77% additional
structural coupling beyond point-mass gravity. Standard models treat
stars as point masses, missing this field origin structure.

Gas does NOT contribute because it is diffuse, not bound into discrete
envelopes with k = 4 closure. Only gravitationally self-bound objects
(stars) have field origins.

C.2  THE EQUATION
------------------
  g_struct(r) = (4/13) · G · M_stellar / R_throat² · [(r − R_t)/(R_env − R_t)]^(3/4)

  For r > R_throat only. Zero for r ≤ R_throat.

C.3  COMPONENT-BY-COMPONENT DERIVATION
-----------------------------------------

  AMPLITUDE: (4/13) · G · M_stellar / R_throat²

    (4/13):          Structural excess. Of the 17 total coupling channels,
                     13 are spatial. The remaining 4/13 is the field origin's
                     topological contribution. This is the "missing structure"
                     that dark matter models attribute to missing mass.

    G · M_stellar:   Only the STELLAR mass participates. Gas is diffuse,
                     has no discrete field origins with k=4 closure.
                     M_stellar = M_bulge + M_disk (excluding M_gas).

    1/R_throat²:     The gravitational acceleration scale at the throat.
                     The structural coupling is normalized to the field
                     strength at the boundary between field origin and
                     spatial projection.

  GROWTH: [(r − R_throat) / (R_env − R_throat)]^(d/k)

    (r − R_t):       Zero at the throat. Structural coupling begins at
                     the throat boundary.

    (R_env − R_t):   Normalizes to the envelope extent. At the horizon
                     (r = R_env), the normalized distance is 1.

    d/k = 3/4:       The fraction of the k-simplex accessible to spatial
                     propagation. The dual tetrad has k = 4 = d+1 faces.
                     Of these, d = 3 face the spatial directions. The
                     structural coupling propagates through 3 spatial
                     dimensions from a field origin embedded in 4-dimensional
                     closure. This ratio IS the growth exponent.

                     NOT FITTED. Derived from d/(d+1) = 3/4.

C.4  STRUCTURAL TERM BEHAVIOR
-------------------------------
  At r = R_throat:  g_struct = 0                          [activates at throat]
  At r = R_env:     g_struct = (4/13) · G·M_star/R_t²    [full amplitude]
  At r > R_env:     g_struct > (4/13) · G·M_star/R_t²    [continues growing]

  The growth is sublinear (exponent 3/4 < 1), so it decelerates.

SECTION D: MASS DISTRIBUTION MODELS
================================================================================

D.1  HERNQUIST PROFILE (BULGE)
-------------------------------
  Density:     ρ(r) = M·a / (2π·r·(r+a)³)
  Enclosed:    M_enc(r) = M · r² / (r + a)²
  Potential:   Φ(r) = −GM / (r + a)
  Parameters:  M = total mass,  a = scale length

  Properties:
    ρ ~ 1/r at small r (cusp)
    ρ ~ r⁻⁴ at large r
    Half-mass radius: r_half = (1 + √2) · a ≈ 2.414 · a
    M_enc(a) = M/4
    M_enc(3a) = 9M/16

D.2  EXPONENTIAL DISK
-----------------------
  Surface density:  Σ(r) = (M / 2π·Rd²) · exp(−r/Rd)
  Enclosed mass:    M_enc(r) = M · [1 − (1 + r/Rd) · exp(−r/Rd)]
  Parameters:       M = total mass,  Rd = scale length

  Properties:
    M_enc(Rd) = 0.264 · M
    M_enc(2·Rd) = 0.594 · M
    M_enc(3·Rd) = 0.801 · M
    M_enc(5·Rd) = 0.960 · M

D.3  SPHERICAL APPROXIMATION
-------------------------------
  Both profiles are evaluated as spherical enclosed mass. For the disk,
  this is a Freeman (1970) thin-disk approximation integrated to
  spherical M_enc. The true disk potential requires solving the 2D
  Poisson equation. The spherical approximation introduces ~5% error
  in the disk-dominated region (1-3 scale lengths). This systematic
  error is smaller than the baryonic mass uncertainty.

  v(r) = √[ g_total(r) · r ]

  g_total(r) = g_grav(r) + g_struct(r)

GRAVITATIONAL TERM:

  Outside field origin (r ≥ R_throat):
    g_grav(r) = g_DTG(r) = ½ [ g_N + √(g_N² + 4·g_N·a₀) ]

    g_struct(r) = 0

  M_enc(r) = from baryonic mass model (observed)

  R_env = baryonic extent (observed)

About GRAVIS

GRAvity VISion

GRAVIS (GRAvity VISion) is a zero-parameter galactic rotation curve prediction tool. Select a galaxy, and GRAVIS computes the rotation curve that the gravity field predicts from baryonic mass alone, with no fitted parameters, no dark matter halo, and no adjustable constants. The result is displayed alongside Newtonian gravity, classical MOND, and the standard dark matter model on a single plot, so every prediction can be compared directly against published observations.

From Topology to Software

The gravity field has internal structure. Two tetrahedral field chambers, coupled at a shared center called the Field Origin, form the topology of the gravitational field at every scale. This topology determines a unique scalar-tensor action with zero free parameters: the Lagrangian, the field equation, the gravitational constant, and the acceleration scale all follow from the coupling hierarchy. The Gravity Field page explains this structure in detail.

GRAVIS is the software implementation of this framework. The path from topology to tool follows four steps:

  • Dual Tetrad Topology establishes the geometric origin: two field chambers coupled at the Field Origin, governed by the coupling polynomial f(k) = 1 + k + k2.
  • Covariant Completion produces a zero-parameter scalar-tensor action in which every physical quantity is derived from the topology.
  • Gravity Field Dynamics (GFD) is the operational formulation: a closed-form, bidirectional field equation that takes mass and predicts velocity, or takes velocity and infers mass.
  • GRAVIS is the modeling engine that evaluates this field equation across ten galaxies, computes rotation curves, infers mass, and validates against observations.

What GRAVIS Computes

For each galaxy, GRAVIS constructs the enclosed baryonic mass from three independently measured components: a Hernquist stellar bulge, an exponential stellar disk, and an exponential gas disk. Stellar masses derive from Spitzer 3.6 μm photometry. Gas masses derive from 21 cm HI observations. No component is fitted to rotation curve data.

Four rotation curves appear on the same plot:

  • Newtonian: the baseline prediction from visible matter alone.
  • GFD (Gravity Field Dynamics): the zero-parameter prediction from the GFD action.
  • Classical MOND: the empirical Milgrom formulation with the same acceleration scale.
  • CDM + NFW: the standard dark matter prediction, fitted to observations when available.

In inference mode, every observation point independently infers a total baryonic mass. Agreement across the full radial range, without parameter tuning, is the non-trivial result.

Capabilities

  • Forward Prediction: compute rotation curves from baryonic mass (M -> v)
  • Inverse Inference: infer baryonic mass from observed velocities (v -> M)
  • Four-Theory Comparison: Newtonian, GFD, MOND, and CDM on a single plot
  • Multi-Point Consistency: per-point residuals, sigma deviations, and shape diagnostics
  • Observational Overlay: published kinematic data with error bars for direct validation
  • Data Provenance: full mass model, observational data, and literature references for each galaxy
  • Ten Galaxies: Milky Way, Andromeda, M33, NGC 3198, NGC 2403, NGC 6503, UGC 2885, IC 2574, DDO 154, NGC 3109
  • REST API: programmatic access to all computations
